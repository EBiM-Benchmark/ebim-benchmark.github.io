Overview

Mobile manipulation, including bimanual coordination, is increasingly combined with foundation models and vision-language-action (VLA) systems. Yet the robotics community still lacks standardized benchmarks that validate these methods in real-world settings. Simulation results often fail to translate to robust deployment due to sim-to-real gaps, platform differences, and limited reproducibility. This workshop proposes a globally coordinated benchmarking initiative through a structured three-phase plan.

Phase I

Open Simulation Benchmark

Release an open simulation benchmark focusing on unstructured manipulation scenarios: cable routing, deformable material handling (thermal pad placement), and assisted living and feeding tasks. Global teams participate through an open call on a shared, reproducible leaderboard.

Phase II

Cross-Continent Real-World Validation

Teams advancing from simulation validate on real Mobile FR3 Duo robots at partner labs in Hamburg, Munich, Pittsburgh, and Shanghai. After simulation results are announced (Aug 8), qualifying teams get a hands-on bench-testing window on the real platform (Aug 10–19). Repository submissions are open now, since the developer-preview release; organizer-run simulation evaluation may begin at any time. From Aug 20–31, all real-robot bench testing and evaluation are run by the organizers — ensuring cross-site comparability.

Phase III

Workshop & Roadmap Release

Competition results, invited talks, and a panel discussion are presented at a community workshop (venue and date TBD). The workshop releases the benchmark protocol, open dataset, and community roadmap for future iterations.

Key Themes

Four research questions at the heart of the EBiM initiative.

Sim-to-Real Transfer

Closing the gap between simulated training and real-world robot performance.

Cross-Site Reproducibility

Ensuring results transfer across labs, hardware, and operator variation worldwide.

Foundation & VLA Model Evaluation

Rigorous benchmarking of vision-language-action models against real manipulation tasks.

Contact-Rich Manipulation

Tasks demanding precise force control, grasping under uncertainty, and deformable object handling.

EBiM Maturity Roadmap

A staged, multi-year path from first edition to long-term community standard — distinct from this year's Phase I/II/III competition timeline.

α
Alpha
2026 · Current
You are here · 2026

The first coordinated edition of EBiM — establishing the core tasks, simulation environments, and cross-continent real-robot validation.

β
Beta
2027 · Target

The next edition — broadening participation, tasks, and testbeds, and hardening protocols based on what we learn in Alpha.

γ
Gamma
Foreseeable future · Goal

A mature, stable benchmark standard maintained as long-term community infrastructure for embodied bimanual manipulation.

EBiM follows a staged, multi-year maturity model inspired by early-access programs such as the PR2 Beta Program.
