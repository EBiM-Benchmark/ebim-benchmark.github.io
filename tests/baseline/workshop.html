Workshop Overview

This half-day workshop (date and venue to be announced) brings together the global mobile manipulation community to present results from the EBiM Competition, hear from leading researchers in real-world bimanual manipulation, and discuss the future of community-driven benchmarking. The workshop is the third phase of our coordinated benchmarking initiative — see the Competition page for benchmark details. Open to the global robotics community.

Discussion Themes
  • Can foundation models enable better generalization in real-world mobile manipulation?
  • How should robustness and standardization be defined in embodied systems?
  • Can cross-site benchmarking become long-term community infrastructure?

Poster Session & Call for Participation

We invite extended-abstract submissions for the workshop poster session — open to academic and industry researchers.

Extended Abstracts

  • 2–4 page extended abstracts (workshop format)
  • Novel tasks, metrics, or datasets
  • Benchmark design and evaluation methodology
  • Bimanual policy learning and transfer
  • Teleoperation and data collection systems

Live Demonstrations

  • Real-robot bimanual manipulation demos
  • Benchmark task reproductions
  • Simulation and digital-twin showcases
  • Teleoperation and data collection pipelines
  • Hardware prototypes and gripper designs

Participation

  • Open attendance — registration details to be announced
  • Poster presenters notified after review
  • Interactive poster & demo session
  • Networking with the bimanual robot community
  • Best Poster Award announced at the workshop

Looking to compete on the benchmark itself? See the Competition page for Phase I (simulation) and Phase II (real-robot validation).
