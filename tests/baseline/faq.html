Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers about the EBiM Challenge 2026. Still stuck? Ask on Discord.

Getting started

How do I register?

Register a team of 1–6 at register.html (中文: /zh/register.html). Member 1 is the point of contact and receives all team email.

Is there an entry fee?

No — participation is free.

Can I compete solo, or do I form my own team?

Teams are 1–6 people; solo is allowed, and you form your own team. Looking for teammates? Use our Discord (and the Shanghai testbed WeChat group for participants in China).

What experience level is required?

All levels are welcome — we provide baseline examples and tutorials.

The competition

What are the tasks?

Three independent tracks — Task 1: Cable Routing & Plugging; Task 2: Deformable Material Handling (Thermal Pad Placement); Task 3: Assisted Living & Feeding. Enter one or more; each is ranked independently.

Which simulators are used?

NVIDIA Isaac Sim and MuJoCo. The MuJoCo version of Task 2 is not in the release (deformable-object fidelity isn't ready yet); if you build your own, it still counts.

What teleoperation interfaces are provided?

To facilitate data collection, the Teleoperation Toolkit provides Franka Duo GELLO (franka.de/de/gello), Keyboard Control, Foot Pedal Control, and VR Control.

Do I need to travel?

The challenge is primarily simulation-based — the open simulation benchmark runs through an open call and needs no travel. Teams that advance from simulation reach real-world validation on Mobile FR3 Duo robots at partner labs in Hamburg, Munich, Pittsburgh, and Shanghai, structured in two windows: a hands-on bench-testing window on the real platform for qualifying teams (Aug 10 – Aug 19, 2026), then an organizer-run window (Aug 20 – Aug 31, 2026) in which teams keep submitting code while the organizers run all real-robot bench testing and evaluation for cross-site comparability.

When are the simulation packages and baselines released?

The first public release is expected within 3–5 days: simulation environments, the Teleoperation Toolkit, benchmark documentation, and baseline examples.

How will submissions be evaluated?

The official rulebook and evaluation metrics are released together with the benchmark package; submission instructions follow.

Compute

What compute can I get, and how do I apply?

Three grant programs, open now at compute-apply.html — Google Cloud (up to US$1,000 of A100; usage Jul 15–20, apply by ~Jul 8), Computational Freedom (40 h RTX 4090 or 25 h L40S; mainland-China-based teams; needs a mainland-China IP and phone number), and AMD Instinct MI300X (shared 8× MI300X in Taipei, all teams). Note: Google Cloud is generally unreachable from mainland China without a VPN — mainland teams should use Computational Freedom or MI300X.

Do I need my own GPU?

Not necessarily — the grants above are intended to cover compute needs.

AMD Solution Award

What is the AMD Solution Award?

A per-task prize for the Top 3 teams that complete the full AI workflow — data processing, training, inference, and official evaluation — on the AMD ROCm ecosystem, verified via your Docker image and the official AMD evaluation environment. It's optional and doesn't affect the overall ranking.

Stay connected

Where do I get help and updates?

Discord, the website, and the WeChat group for participants in China.
